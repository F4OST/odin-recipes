Falafel Recipe

What Is Falafel:

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern food that consists of small balls or patties made from ground chickpeas or fava beans
mixed with herbs and spices, and then deep-fried or baked. It is typically served in pita bread
or on a plate with a variety of toppings and sauces, such as tahini hummus, or tzatziki.
Falafel is a vegetarian and veganfood that is enjoyed for its delicious taste and its reputation as a healthy food choice.

Ingredients:

  • 1cup dried chickpeas
  • 1/2onion, chopped
  • 1/4cup fresh parsley leaves
  • 1/4cup fresh cliantro leaves
  • 1teaspoon salt
  • 1teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 4cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1teaspoon baking powder
  • 4tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Oli for frying

Instructions:

  1. Soak the dried chickpeas in cold water overnight, or for at least 6 hours.
  2. Drain the chickpeas and place them in a food processor along with the chopped onion,parsley, cilantro, salt, cumin, coriander, black pepper, and garlic.
  3. Pulse the mixture until it is finely ground but not pureed.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and stir in the baking powder and enough flour to make a dough that holds together.
  5. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  6. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat.
  7. Form the falafel mixture into small balls or patties, about 1-2 tablespoons each.
  8. Fry the falafel until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  9. Serve hot with pita bread, hummus, and your favorite toppings.
